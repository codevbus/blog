### Who am I?
I'm Mike Vanbuskirk. I'm a system engineer based out of Seattle, Washington(near where the picture of the rocks came from). I currently live(and telecommute) in Western Michigan with my wife, son, 4 cats and a dog. 

Blogging is something I've tried before, but I often found myself losing interest in writing, and the blog would languish. This time around, I'm hoping to use the blog as more of a tool, rather than a one-way speaking podium. I want it to function as an aggregator of anything that is interesting, insightful, clever, functional, or helpful in the world of DevOps. I'm more than willing to write about it if it's the opposite of these things as well. 

### Why?
The space we work in is iterating at an incredibly rapid pace. I often find myself struggling to stay informed of the current DevOps *meta*, all of the new tools, paradigms and stacks which evolve at a dizzying rate. We often work with problems on which very little has been written, and it seems that new territory is constantly being forged. I'm very grateful for what *has* been done and written about, and it has been instrumental as a tool for my own work and development. I'd like to think I'll be able to contribute at least a few drops back to the trickle of information available, in the hope that it one day becomes a very robust river.
