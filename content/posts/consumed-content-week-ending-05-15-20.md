## Summary {#summary}

This was a pretty solid week in terms of listening to podcasts. Most of my listening has been focused on the Indie Hackers podcast, which is a part of a larger online community called [Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/). Definitely worth checking out if you're interested in starting your own online business, marketing, side hustles etc...

I stil find it easier to make time for listening versus reading, as I can do the former when I'm exercising or making food. Reading requires extended focus and stillness if I want to really retain what I'm reading.

I particularly enjoyed the synchrony of two of the articles I did manage to read, both tackling, ostensibly, the idea of procrastination.

Also, as a remote worker, it brings a smile to my face to see a "big" tech company go full WFH. Will others follow?

Hopefully everyone is staying healthy and safe. Enjoy your weekend.


### Podcasts {#podcasts}

-   [#161 – How to Make Millions by Writing Online with Sam Parr of The Hustle](https://www.indiehackers.com/podcast/161-sam-parr-of-the-hustle) - Sam Parr is definitely "in your face", but he strikes me as someone worth listening to. Particularly noteworthy to me was that he believes his primary revenue stream will be subscription-based, and not ad-based, which is a theme I'd like to see replicated elsewhere.
-   [#145 – The Slow, Deliberate Process of Making a SaaS Business Work with Jane Portman of Userlist](https://www.indiehackers.com/podcast/145-jane-portman-of-userlist) - "Productization" is something I've been interested in recently, in terms of having a repeatable way to generate income. Once upon a time I did consulting, and it didn't take very long for my time to be consumed with non-revenue generating administrative minutiae.
-   [#155 – Finding the Right Models for Growth with Brian Balfour of Reforge](https://www.indiehackers.com/podcast/155-brian-balfour-of-reforge) - I liked the discussion around growth being a series of "feedback loops". I also like "do the opposite" of what everyone else is doing.
-   [#157 – Listening to Users and Growing to 100,000 MRR with Baird Hall of Wavve](https://www.indiehackers.com/podcast/157-baird-hall-of-wavve) - It's always inspiring to hear someone failing at something, and then sticking it out to ultimately succeed, and that's the story here.
-   [#114 – The Business of Podcasting with Jeff Meyerson of Software Engineering Daily](https://www.indiehackers.com/podcast/114-jeff-meyerson-of-software-engineering-daily) - I really enjoy the SEDaily podcast, and I'm fortunate to actually work alongside one of Jeff's mentors. This was one of those episodes where I just got to hear how someone I enjoy listening to thinks.
-   [#149 – Generating Passive Income by Teaching What You Know with Greg Rog of LearnUX](https://www.indiehackers.com/podcast/149-greg-rog-of-learnux) - I found a really useful tool from this podcast: <https://integromat.com>. The amount of automation this enables with 0 code is pretty powerful. I built a rather dumb social metrics tracker in 2 hours. It would've taken a lot longer doing it in code, by hand.
-   [Thinking About Thinking with Tyler Cowen {The Knowledge Project Ep. #39}](https://fs.blog/knowledge-project/tyler-cowen/) - KP episodes are great, since they focus on thinking and mental models. This episode gave me a new website to read(<https://marginalrevolution.com>), and had some great discussion on reading. I was surprised to learn he maybe reads 1 in 10 books. Also, good books shouldn't surprise you.
-   [Eric Weinstein: Geometric Unity and the Call for New Ideas, Leaders & Institutions | MIT | Artificial Intelligence Podcast](https://lexfridman.com/eric-weinstein-2/) - "Geometric Unity" is an attempt at a unified theory to explain the macro and quantum worlds in one swipe. I also gleaned there's no love lost between Eric and big educational institutions either.


### Articles/Blogs {#articles-blogs}

-   [Ugh fields](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/EFQ3F6kmt4WHXRqik/ugh-fields) - This was an enjoyable, short take on why humans generally avoid doing unpleasant things. At the end of the article were some potential mitigation strategies. This also happens to be a segue into...
-   [Exploiting Productivity Momentum](https://commoncog.com/blog/exploiting-productivity-momentum/) - ... the next article. It's easier to continue than it is to start something. I've found myself putting off working on difficult technical items because it "hurt" too much to not know what I was doing. Key takeaway: start harder stuff during high motivational periods.
-   [The Machine Pauses](https://hedgehogreview.com/blog/thr/posts/the-machine-pauses) - Efficiency for our own sake, or for that of the "machine"? This echoes some other fatalist tropes I've seen about technology and the effect it has on our society. Just like newspapers and magazines ruined books.
-   [Twitter says staff can continue working from home permanently](https://techcrunch.com/2020/05/12/twitter-says-staff-can-continue-working-from-home-permanently/) - Remote work FTW.
