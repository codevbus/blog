## Summary {#summary}

Unfortunately, another week of lagging reading. I did manage to read the Firecracker paper from AWS, which really impressed me. As always, I was lucky enough to have time for some interesting podcasts. SE Daily did a week of Javascript, so I spent some time with how the "other half" lives :) The AI podcast has had some great guests discussing topics in physics and quantum computing as well, and it's still one of my most listened-to podcasts at the moment.

Obviously looming large this week is the Coronavirus, otherwise designated as COVID-19. My only hope is that everyone takes this as seriously as it warrants, and that preventative measures _now_ avert a bigger disaster later.

Stay safe and healthy this weekend.


### Podcasts {#podcasts}

-   [Lee Smolin: Quantum Gravity and Einstein's Unfinished Revolution | MIT | Artificial Intelligence Podcast](https://lexfridman.com/lee-smolin/) - This was another podcast that went over my head and times, but had some incredible discussions around the nature of what's real, and the scientific method.
-   [Scott Aaronson: Quantum Computing | MIT | Artificial Intelligence Podcast](https://lexfridman.com/scott-aaronson/) - This had one of the most digestible, easy to understand descriptions of quantum computing, the problem spaces, and the current state-of-the-art.
-   [React Fundamentals with Ryan Florence - Software Engineering Daily](https://softwareengineeringdaily.com/2020/03/03/react-fundamentals-with-ryan-florence/) - Part of the Javascript week at SE Daily. I've always wanted to learn more about life in frontend land, so this series was timely.
-   [JavaScript Deployments with Brian LeRoux - Software Engineering Daily](https://softwareengineeringdaily.com/2020/03/04/javascript-deployments-with-brian-leroux/) - Another in the Javascript series. The guest makes a powerful case for leaning on the heavy abstractions in the JAMStack.
-   [React Stack with Tejas Kumar - Software Engineering Daily](https://softwareengineeringdaily.com/2020/03/05/react-stack-with-tejas-kumar/) - A React deep dive, another from the SE Daily Javascript series.


### Articles/Blogs {#articles-blogs}

-   [Always Be Journaling](https://letterstoanewdeveloper.com/2018/12/14/always-be-journaling/) - Another post confirming(reinforcing?) the idea of consistent writing and journaling as a way to solidify your ideas, make new connections, and improve your skillset.
-   [Do whatever you can't stop thinking about](https://shime.sh/do-whatever-you-can%27t-stop-thinking-about) - A short, powerful post about following your passions and having no regrets. I can definitely identify with the feeling about something I want to work on, versus something I _have_ to.


### Papers {#papers}

-   [Firecracker: lightweight virtualization for serverless applications](https://blog.acolyer.org/2020/03/02/firecracker/) - As a huge fan of AWS Lambda, this paper held a special interest for me. This provides some amazing insight into how an AWS engineering team designs a service that serves millions of users and trillions of requests.
