## Summary {#summary}

Week 1 of a new "normal" ends today. These are extraordinary times. I hope everyone is finding ways to stay safe, healthy, and sane right now.

I find myself with a lot less time for podcast listening and reading. Ideally, I can find some way to rebalance in the coming weeks. I've got several things in flight right now, but without meaningful chunks of time in which to finish them.


### Articles/Blogs {#articles-blogs}

-   [You’re Not Managing a Team of Software Engineers, You’re Managing a Team of Writers](https://medium.com/coaching-notes/youre-not-managing-a-team-of-software-engineers-you-re-managing-a-team-of-writers-b263d3a10cc7) - A good article that draws the comparison of managing a team of coders to being more akin to managing a team of writers. It raises some thoughtful points on balancing the tension of allowing the time for creative ideas to develop versus "buckling down" and getting work done.
-   [How to Write Usefully](http://paulgraham.com/useful.html) - One of the best things I've read about writing, by Paul Graham. He lays out a formula for a good essay: importance + novelty + correctness + strength, and argues that while you should write a lot, very little should pass through the filter of publication.


### Videos {#videos}

-   [How to Take Smart Notes | Zettelkasten Method in Roam Research - YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ljyo%5FWAJevQ) - This is a short video that does a great job of explaining the Zettelkasten note taking method in the context of the Roam software. I use an [emulated form of Roam](https://github.com/jethrokuan/org-roam) in my IDE, and it's proving to be very helpful.
